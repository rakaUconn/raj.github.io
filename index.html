Research Projects

Meibomian Gland Imaging Using Diffraction Phase Imaging

This research focuses on utilizing diffraction phase imaging to study and diagnose Meibomian Gland Dysfunction (MGD) at an early stage. The project integrates optics, machine learning, and computational techniques to enhance diagnostic capabilities in ophthalmology.

Techniques: Diffraction Phase Imaging, Optical Tomography, Machine Learning

Collaborators: University of Houston

Optical Signal Detection in Turbid Water

Developed an optical signal detection system using 3D integral imaging and deep learning to enhance signal clarity in turbid water environments. This project tackled challenges such as occlusion and light scattering in underwater conditions.

Techniques: 3D Integral Imaging, Deep Learning, Polarimetric Optics

Outcome: Published in Optics Express

Multimodal Micro-Endoscope Development

Worked on developing a multimodal micro-endoscope for bright-field and fluorescence imaging. The device is capable of examining biological cells and tissues for ex vivo and in vivo applications. Collaborated with medical professionals and engineers for cross-disciplinary research.

Techniques: Optical Microscopy, Fluorescence Imaging, Biomedical Engineering

Collaborators: IIT Delhi, AIIMS New Delhi
